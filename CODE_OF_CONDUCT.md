# PactRide Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

PactRide welcomes drivers, riders, developers, researchers, designers, operators, critics, and people with different political or economic views. Participation depends on constructive conduct, not agreement with the project's premise.

## Expected behavior

- Discuss evidence, designs, and tradeoffs directly.
- Distinguish protocol facts from opinion and local policy.
- Treat lived transportation experience as relevant expertise.
- Protect private identity, location, and trip information.
- Disclose conflicts of interest.
- Credit prior art and contributors.
- Accept correction when claims are unsupported.
- Keep criticism specific and actionable.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, stalking, or discriminatory abuse.
- Publishing private identities, exact locations, trip histories, or security details without consent.
- Sexualized conduct or unwanted personal attention.
- Retaliation against reporters or reviewers.
- Deliberate misinformation about safety, law, licensing, or production readiness.
- Token promotion, investment solicitation, or paid influence disguised as technical contribution.
- Attempts to use the project to target drivers, riders, communities, or protected groups.

## Safety-sensitive discussion

Examples and test fixtures must use fictional identities and locations. Do not post live ride requests, identity documents, phone numbers, license plates, or exact residential coordinates.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content; issue warnings; temporarily restrict participation; or permanently ban contributors. Serious privacy or security incidents may be preserved privately for investigation.

## Maintainer accountability

Maintainers are subject to the same rules. Enforcement decisions should be documented when privacy and safety permit. A maintainer with a conflict must recuse themselves.

## Reporting

For ordinary conduct issues, open a concise issue or contact a maintainer. For sensitive privacy, stalking, or security matters, follow `SECURITY.md` and do not publish harmful details.

## Scope

This code applies to repository issues, pull requests, discussions, project communication channels, events, and public representation of the project.
