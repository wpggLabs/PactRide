# PactRide Contributor Licensing Policy

## Repository-wide contribution terms

PactRide uses the **Apache License 2.0** for the repository. Unless a file contains a different explicit notice, contributions to protocol documents, schemas, test vectors, examples, website files, automation, and software are submitted under Apache-2.0.

Section 5 of Apache-2.0 states that a contribution intentionally submitted for inclusion is provided under the license without additional terms unless the contributor explicitly states otherwise or a separate written agreement applies.

## Contributor responsibilities

By submitting a contribution, the contributor is responsible for confirming that:

- the work is original or properly authorized;
- they have the right to submit it under Apache-2.0;
- employer, client, school, or contractual ownership does not prevent submission;
- third-party code, text, data, media, and licenses are disclosed;
- modified third-party files retain required notices;
- no private keys, identity documents, real trip histories, exact personal travel data, or confidential information are included;
- significant AI assistance is disclosed when it creates provenance or licensing uncertainty;
- generated material has been reviewed for copied or incompatible content.

## Corporate contributions

When an employer or client owns a contribution, the submitter must have authority to contribute it. Maintainers may request written confirmation or a corporate contribution process when ownership is unclear.

## No automatic commercial entitlement

Contributing does not make a contributor, maintainer, or founder entitled to revenue generated by another Apache-2.0 user. Funding, employment, sponsorship, consulting, hosting, certification, or other commercial arrangements require separate agreements.

## Trademarks

Apache-2.0 does not grant general permission to market a product as official PactRide software or as certified by the project. See [`TRADEMARK.md`](TRADEMARK.md).

## Developer Certificate of Origin

PactRide does not currently require a contributor license agreement. All contributions must use `git commit -s`. By adding the `Signed-off-by` line, the contributor certifies that they wrote the contribution or have the right to submit it under Apache-2.0, consistent with the Developer Certificate of Origin 1.1. Corporate contribution terms may be added later after legal review; they cannot be implied retroactively.

## Rejected contributions

Maintainers may reject contributions that:

- cannot be distributed under Apache-2.0;
- copy incompatible material;
- contain secrets or personal data;
- obscure provenance;
- introduce hidden telemetry or mandatory centralized services;
- make unsupported safety, legal, privacy, reliability, or decentralization claims.
