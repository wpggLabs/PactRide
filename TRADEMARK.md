# PactRide Trademark Policy

## Status

This is an interim project policy, not a trademark registration or legal opinion. It should be reviewed before PactRide launches an official client, certification program, commercial service, or fundraising campaign.

## Purpose

Open source permits broad use of software and specifications. It does not require the project to allow misleading use of its name or visual identity.

This policy aims to:

- let people truthfully describe compatibility with the PactRide protocol;
- prevent forks and services from falsely implying official approval;
- preserve the ability to create an official compatibility or security program;
- keep trademark control separate from technical control of the protocol.

## Marks covered

This policy applies to:

- the name **PactRide**;
- the PactRide wordmark and visual identity;
- future PactRide logos;
- future marks such as **PactRide Compatible**, **PactRide Verified**, or similar certification labels.

Copyright and open-source licenses do not grant trademark rights in these marks.

## Uses allowed without separate permission

Subject to applicable law, you may generally:

- refer to the PactRide project or protocol accurately;
- link to the official repository or website;
- state that software “implements the PactRide protocol”;
- state that software is “compatible with PactRide” when the statement is technically accurate and does not imply certification;
- discuss, review, criticize, teach, or report on PactRide;
- identify an unmodified copy of official PactRide material;
- use the name in source-code comments or dependency descriptions where needed for identification.

Such use should not be more prominent than your own product or organization name.

## Uses requiring permission

Do not use the PactRide marks without written permission to:

- name a product, company, hosted service, domain, application, token, fundraiser, or organization;
- imply that a fork or deployment is the official PactRide implementation;
- claim certification, verification, endorsement, partnership, sponsorship, or approval;
- use the marks in an app-store listing as the primary product name;
- sell merchandise primarily branded with PactRide;
- register confusingly similar trademarks, domains, social handles, or organization names;
- create a logo or visual identity likely to be confused with the official project.

## Forks and modified versions

Forks are allowed under the applicable software license, but materially modified distributions should:

1. use a distinct product name;
2. state clearly that they are independent and not endorsed by PactRide;
3. preserve required copyright and license notices;
4. avoid the official visual identity;
5. describe protocol compatibility truthfully.

A statement such as “ExampleClient implements the PactRide protocol and is not affiliated with the PactRide project” is preferred.

## Compatibility and certification

PactRide does not currently operate an official certification program.

A future program may charge for testing, audit work, support, or use of a certification mark. The underlying public specification and conformance tests should remain available so certification does not become a hidden technical permission system.

No product should use “PactRide Certified,” “Official PactRide,” or a similar claim until a formal program and written permission exist.

## Community use

Community groups, meetups, translations, and research efforts may request permission to use the name. Approval may require a clear statement that the activity is community-run and not an official transportation service.

## Safety-sensitive use

Permission may be refused or withdrawn when use of the marks creates a material risk of misleading people about:

- production readiness;
- identity verification;
- insurance;
- emergency response;
- legal or regulatory status;
- payment protection;
- security review;
- compatibility with the published protocol.

## Requesting permission

Open a GitHub issue titled `Trademark permission request` and include:

- the requesting person or organization;
- the proposed use;
- images or mockups;
- where the mark will appear;
- whether money will be collected;
- the requested duration and territories;
- any relevant safety or compatibility claims.

Do not include confidential business information in a public issue. Ask maintainers for a private contact method when needed.

## No effect on protocol implementation

This policy does not prevent anyone from independently implementing the PactRide protocol or truthfully describing technical compatibility. It governs branding and claims of official association, not protocol access.
