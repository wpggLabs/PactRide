# PactRide Non-Goals

PactRide is deliberately narrow. The project will fail if every adjacent transportation problem is pulled into the base protocol.

## Not an official global rideshare company

PactRide does not employ drivers, set universal prices, guarantee rides, operate a call center, or own a worldwide marketplace.

## Not a Bluetooth-only citywide network

Nearby-device networking cannot reliably provide city-scale discovery on its own. Bluetooth and Wi-Fi Direct are fallback transports and verification tools, not the only network.

## Not a blockchain project

The base protocol does not require a chain, token, DAO, NFT, mining system, or speculative asset. Bilateral signed events and replaceable relays are sufficient for the initial coordination problem.

## Not universal identity verification

The protocol will not declare one global authority for government identity, licensing, criminal history, insurance, or vehicle inspection. It may carry signed attestations issued by communities or external organizations.

## Not a universal reputation score

PactRide should preserve evidence and context. It should not reduce every person to a single globally authoritative number.

## Not mandatory anonymous participation

Privacy is not the same as unaccountability. Communities may require attestations or known membership. The base protocol supports pseudonymous keys but does not promise that every ride can or should be anonymous.

## Not emergency-response infrastructure

No PactRide client should claim to replace emergency services. Safety features may help users share information or preserve evidence, but availability and response cannot be guaranteed by the protocol.

## Not a global payment system

PactRide may represent agreed fares, receipts, and optional settlement proofs. It does not attempt to become a bank, escrow provider, currency, chargeback system, or universal dispute court.

## Not a guarantee against law or platform pressure

Open source and decentralized transport reduce single points of control; they do not make participants immune to local law, device restrictions, app-store policy, network blocking, or physical coercion.

## Not optimized for maximum transaction volume at any cost

Privacy, transparent trust, portability, and safe failure take priority over aggressive growth or engagement metrics.

## Not dependent on one map provider

Clients may use OpenStreetMap, commercial mapping, locally cached maps, or external navigation apps. The protocol should exchange coordinates and route-relevant metadata without prescribing a map vendor.

## Not a finished safety model

The repository is a research RFC. It must not imply that cryptography solves assault, fraud, discrimination, accessibility, insurance, physical safety, or dispute resolution.
