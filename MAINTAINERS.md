# PactRide Maintainers

## Current stewardship

- **Current maintainer:** `wpggLabs`
- **Project status:** pre-implementation research RFC
- **Repository:** https://github.com/wpggLabs/PactRide

The founder role records who started the project. It does not make the founder a permanent protocol authority, transportation operator, or owner of every compatible implementation.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- preserving the protocol-before-platform vision;
- keeping public claims aligned with actual evidence;
- reviewing protocol, privacy, safety, accessibility, and interoperability changes;
- maintaining schemas, test vectors, documentation, website content, and validation workflows;
- protecting the project from hidden mandatory infrastructure or protocol taxes;
- disclosing relevant conflicts of interest;
- responding to security reports within the limits stated in `SECURITY.md`;
- documenting unresolved disagreement rather than silently rewriting project history.

Maintainers do not certify that PactRide is safe, lawful, insured, production-ready, or suitable for public transportation operations.

## Adding maintainers

A contributor may be invited to become a maintainer after demonstrating sustained, constructive work in one or more of these areas:

- protocol and interoperability engineering;
- security or privacy research;
- mobile or relay implementation;
- accessibility and inclusive transportation design;
- driver, rider, cooperative, municipal, or nonprofit operations;
- governance, legal, or standards work.

Admission should consider:

1. quality and consistency of contributions;
2. ability to separate evidence from aspiration;
3. respect for privacy, safety, and accessibility boundaries;
4. willingness to document tradeoffs and dissent;
5. absence of undisclosed conflicts that would compromise stewardship.

New maintainers should be recorded in this file through a public repository change.

## Decision authority

While there is one active maintainer, the current maintainer may merge or reject changes, but significant protocol changes should remain documented through issues or RFCs.

As maintainer diversity grows:

- routine documentation and test changes may be approved by one maintainer;
- normative protocol or governance changes should receive review from at least two maintainers when practical;
- safety-sensitive, privacy-sensitive, licensing, trademark, and production-readiness claims should receive domain review before adoption;
- unresolved objections should be recorded in the relevant issue or RFC.

## Inactive maintainers

A maintainer may be marked inactive after six months without repository participation or after explicitly stepping down. Inactive maintainers retain credit but should not be counted toward active-review requirements.

Reactivation requires a public update to this file.

## Succession and continuity

The project should remain transferable if the founder stops maintaining it.

A future transition should, where legally and technically possible, include:

- repository administration;
- GitHub Pages and domain control;
- release and package credentials;
- security contact access;
- project email and social accounts;
- trademark and certification records, if any;
- funding, sponsorship, or organizational records, if any.

No successor automatically receives personal accounts, private data, or rights that the founder does not legally control.

## Emergency changes

A maintainer may make a narrow emergency change without the normal review window when necessary to remove exposed secrets, personal data, malicious content, or a clearly dangerous security claim. The reason and scope should be documented afterward.

## Contact

Use GitHub issues or discussions for normal project matters. Follow `SECURITY.md` for vulnerability reports. Do not place private keys, identity documents, exact trip data, or confidential legal information in a public issue.
