# PactRide Monetization Boundaries

PactRide is not designed to charge a commission on every ride or make protocol access dependent on one company. Revenue should come from optional value created around the open project, not ownership of the protocol idea itself.

## Compatible revenue

- Managed relay and deployment services.
- Security, privacy, accessibility, and interoperability audits.
- Integration and implementation consulting.
- Paid support and service-level agreements.
- Training and operator onboarding.
- Compatibility testing and authorized use of a certification mark.
- Grants, sponsorships, and donations.
- Official applications or operations products built on the open protocol.
- Paid commercial licenses for future separately licensed official software when PactRide controls the required rights.
- Partnerships with cooperatives, universities, nonprofits, municipalities, and commercial operators.

## Revenue the protocol should not require

- Mandatory per-ride commission.
- A required token or platform wallet.
- Payment to publish or receive base protocol events.
- Permission to create an independent compatible client.
- Use of one required relay, map, payment processor, identity provider, or hosted API.
- A royalty or revenue share from Apache-2.0 use or an independent implementation of the public specification.
- Payment merely because someone applies the underlying coordination idea without copying separately licensed software or branding.

## Founder economics

The founder can potentially earn money by building and maintaining valuable software, trademarks, services, expertise, relationships, and operational infrastructure.

Apache-2.0 does not create an automatic right to payment when another party uses, modifies, distributes, or commercializes PactRide. AGPL software licensing can require source availability in covered circumstances, but it also does not create an automatic royalty from every user or independent protocol implementation.

The strongest realistic position is to become the trusted steward and a strong optional service provider—not a mandatory technical gatekeeper.

## Official software strategy

The current repository is the open PactRide specification and research record.

Future official rider applications, driver applications, operator tools, hosted services, or relay products should normally use separate repositories with explicit licenses. Where PactRide owns sufficient rights, official software could be offered under an open-source license and a separate paid commercial license.

That model monetizes the official software and associated services. It does not convert the Apache-2.0 protocol specification into a royalty-bearing standard or prevent clean-room compatible implementations.

See [`LICENSING.md`](LICENSING.md) for the detailed boundary and contributor-rights requirements.

## Trademark and certification value

The PactRide name, logo, official endorsement, and any future certification mark are separate from protocol compatibility.

A third party may truthfully describe a compatible implementation, but authorized official branding, certification, partnership status, or endorsement may support paid programs when governed transparently and reviewed appropriately.

See [`TRADEMARK.md`](TRADEMARK.md).

## Separation rule

Paid services must be disclosed clearly and remain replaceable. The protocol, schemas, test vectors, and conformance process should remain usable without purchasing hosting, consulting, certification, or an official application from PactRide maintainers.
